# NeuroHID Decision Brief (1-Page)

**Date:** 2026-02-14  
**Topic:** Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI)  
**Decision Goal:** Should NeuroHID proceed now, and where should it focus first?

## Executive Decision

**Proceed now with a focused platform strategy.**  
NeuroHID should position as an **interoperability and control layer** across heterogeneous BCI devices, starting with **assistive communication/control workflows** where value is clearest and outcomes are measurable.

## Why This Matters Now

- BCI is moving from research-centric usage to early real-world deployment in clinical assistive and consumer/prosumer contexts.
- Market direction is consistently positive across sources (despite variance in exact TAM/CAGR values).
- Neurological burden remains high, supporting persistent unmet need for assistive neurotechnology.
- Regulatory expectations are maturing quickly (quality, cybersecurity, AI lifecycle governance), favoring teams that operationalize trust and compliance early.

## Evidence Snapshot

- **Market trajectory:** Growth is strong directionally; estimates vary by methodology, so use ranges, not a single point estimate.
- **Segment reality:** Non-invasive pathways dominate current scale; software/algorithm value is rising.
- **Competitive structure:** Fragmented ecosystem with no universal cross-modality control standard.
- **Regulatory signal:** U.S./EU/UK pathways increasingly emphasize lifecycle controls, postmarket monitoring, and cybersecurity resilience.

## Strategic Positioning for NeuroHID

**Recommended role:** Neutral, modality-agnostic BCI middleware/control plane.

Core value:

1. Normalize heterogeneous neural signals into stable intent abstractions.
2. Reduce integration cost/time for app and device partners.
3. Provide compliance-native telemetry and change-governance evidence.
4. Improve trust through privacy-by-design and security-by-design controls.

## 90-Day Priority Plan

1. **Claims boundary + compliance matrix** (U.S./EU/UK) tied to product scope.
2. **V1 canonical intent API** and integration reference architecture.
3. **Two pilot tracks** in assistive communication/control with defined success metrics.
4. **Governance baseline**: model versioning, rollback, drift triggers, release gates.
5. **Partner pipeline**: initiate interoperability pilots with device and app vendors.

## Key Risks to Manage

- Signal robustness in uncontrolled real-world conditions.
- Regulatory scope drift from product messaging/claims.
- AI/model change without disciplined controls.
- Privacy and cybersecurity failures involving neural-adjacent data.
- Ecosystem fragmentation and proprietary lock-in.

## Go / No-Go Criteria

**Go (now)** if NeuroHID commits to:

- Platform-first architecture (not single-device dependence)
- Measured assistive-use pilots
- Compliance and security embedded in product velocity

**No-Go / Delay** if strategy depends on:

- Unverified mass-market assumptions
- Late-stage compliance retrofitting
- Single-vendor ecosystem bets

## Bottom Line

The opportunity is real and timely.  
**NeuroHID should launch as the trusted interoperability layer for practical BCI adoption, beginning with high-value assistive workflows and compliance-native execution.**
