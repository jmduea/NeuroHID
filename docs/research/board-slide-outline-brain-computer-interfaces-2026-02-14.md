**Audience:** Board / Executive Leadership

## Slide 2 — Why Now

**Title:** Market + Need + Timing Convergence  
**Core message:** Clinical need, market momentum, and regulatory maturity have aligned.

**Include:**

- BCI transition from lab to early real-world deployment
- Persistent neurological unmet need (high burden)
- Growth direction positive despite forecast variance
- Regulatory systems now clearer for software-rich devices

**Presenter cue:** Emphasize directional certainty over single-number precision.

## Slide 3 — Evidence Snapshot

**Title:** What the Research Confirms  
**Core message:** Structural signals support a platform play.

**Include:**

- Non-invasive pathways dominate current scale
- Software/algorithm layer increasing strategic value
- Competitive field fragmented (no universal standard)
- Privacy/cybersecurity/governance now procurement-critical

**Presenter cue:** Tie each point to strategic implications, not just observations.

---

## Slide 4 — Strategic Positioning

**Title:** NeuroHID’s Winning Position  
**Core message:** Be the modality-agnostic control plane, not a single-device product.

**Include:**

- Normalize heterogeneous neural signals into intent abstractions
- Reduce partner integration time/cost via SDK/API
- Deliver compliance-native telemetry and change governance
- Build trust through privacy/security by design

**Presenter cue:** Clarify how this avoids direct hardware commodity competition.

---

## Slide 5 — 90-Day Execution Plan

**Title:** Immediate Plan (0–90 Days)  
**Core message:** Fast, measurable progress with controlled risk.

**Include:**

1. Claims boundary + jurisdictional compliance matrix (US/EU/UK)
2. V1 canonical intent API + integration architecture
3. Two assistive pilot tracks with measurable outcomes
4. Governance baseline (model versioning, rollback, drift triggers)
5. Partner pipeline initiation (device + app ecosystem)

**Presenter cue:** Show this as a de-risking plan, not just a build plan.

---

## Slide 6 — Risks and Mitigations

**Title:** Top Risks and How We Contain Them  
**Core message:** Risks are real but manageable with disciplined operating controls.

**Include:**

- Signal robustness risk → staged pilots, cohort QA, fallback controls
- Regulatory scope drift → claim governance and release gate review
- AI change risk → strict change-control policy and monitoring thresholds
- Privacy/cyber risk → minimum-data architecture + security baseline
- Ecosystem lock-in risk → open integration interfaces and partner diversity

**Presenter cue:** Frame risk management as a strategic differentiator.

---

## Slide 7 — Go / No-Go Framework

**Title:** Decision Gates  
**Core message:** Proceed under explicit operating conditions.

**Include:**
**Go if:**

- Platform-first architecture is maintained
- Assistive pilots produce measurable efficacy/reliability
- Compliance/security embedded in delivery lifecycle

**Delay if:**

- Strategy drifts to unvalidated mass-market assumptions
- Compliance remains late-stage retrofit
- Single-vendor dependence emerges

**Presenter cue:** Reinforce governance discipline and capital efficiency.

**Title:** Approval Request + 2-Quarter Success Metrics

- Approve 90-day plan and partner pilot budget envelope
- Endorse compliance-first product governance model

**Quarterly success metrics:**

- Pilot reliability and user task-completion outcomes

- Security/privacy control readiness score
- Regulatory readiness artifacts completed on schedule

**Presenter cue:** Close with confidence: right market, right position, right sequencing.
